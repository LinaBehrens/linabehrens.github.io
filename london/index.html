Lina's London tips

I came to London back in September 2010, quickly fell in love with this marvellous, bustling city and ended up staying much longer than I would have ever thought. Now, six years and many wonderful moments later, I decided to leave London, my home. But as many good memories remain, I thought I'd capture some of my favourite spots for you. I hope you'll enjoy them - do let me know if so!

As you might guess, I won't be able to update this list very frequently (and have certainly missed many great spots!). So please feel free to post any of your own recommendations in the comments below!

Things To Do
  • walk along Regent’s canal – both east and west of King’s Cross
  • Take a pick nick to Hampstead Heath and swim in the ponds
  • Enjoy the skyline view from Primrose Hill
  • Have breakfast at Maltby Street Market (weekend)
  • Stroll around Broadway Market (Saturday only)
  • Visit Columbia Road Flower Market
  • Enjoy great concerts by Multi-Storey Orchestra in the car park below Frank’s Bar in Peckham
  • Explore East London’s bars and gins during the fantastic Shake Rattle and Stir Gin tour
  • Or visit Jensen Gin distillery for some tasting
  • Join a Street Art Tour and get to know unseen parts of East London
  • See London through different eyes through Unseen Tours working with homeless, ex-homeless and vulnerably housed Londoners
  • Explore one of the museums during a Friday Late, e.g. The V&A, or spend a full day exploring Tate Modern
  • Or visit Damian Hirst’s Newport Street Gallery
  • Go to one of the events @ Vault Festival
  • Get into the Xmas mood by joining one of Miriam’s Karolaoke evenings (and nudge her to organise another one, it’s just not a real Christmas without it!)
  • Visit one of the Hampton Court Palace Summer Nights for a truly British evening
  • Or even better, secure tickets to one of the Somerset House summer concerts (advance tickets available if you sign up with an AmEx!)
  • Go promming for some world class music @The BBC Proms
  • Join Kristian for a 20-20 cricket match…
  • …or for one of Dulwich Hamlet’s football plays
  • Check out one of the many film festivals (especially BFI in October) or have a look at what’s on at the Ritzy in Brixton
  • Visit Kew Gardens
  • Take a boat to Greenwich
  • Go kayaking on the Thames next to Hampton Court Palace
  • Gather a team and compete at Crystal Maze
Day trips
  • Take a train to Margate, explore the Contemporary Turner Museum and walk along a beautiful coastal path to Broadstairs (for more detailed infos)
  • Take a train to Whitstable, enjoy the sea and a delicious fish for lunch, and head on to Canterbury to visit the stunning cathedral
  • Walk from Hever to Leigh: During only 3h40 of actual walking, you see 2 castles, a stately home and it all sounds lovely. You can get there easily by train and there’s a pretty good pub half-way… (for more detailed infos)
  • Get up early and enjoy a Hot Air Balloon ride in Surrey
  • Take a day to explore the stunning colleges at Oxford and end the day at one of the oldest pubs, the Bear Inn
  • Visit the coastal town Hastings
  • Go hiking in Seven sisters
Weekend trips
  • Hike along Hadrian’s Wall and explore Newcastle (make sure not to miss the local G&T in Hexam)
  • Spend at least a weekend in both the Peak District and the Lake District
  • Go hiking in lovely Wales – I truly enjoyed both Pembrokshire and Snowdonia
  • Explore the New Forest
  • Leave London and enjoy a wonderful weekend Wilderness Festival
Other tips for London’s busy social life
  • Get £20 tickets for the subsequent week (even for sold out shows): Friday rush @ National Theatre
  • Sign up to TodayTix and participate in their lottery
  • Explore the sheer variety of London’s bars and restaurant scene: The Nudge Membership (or at least sign up for their free newsletter)
  • Get discounts at museums all across the country: National Art Pass
  • Indulge in some of the world’s best exhibitions as often as you like: @ Tate with an annual Membership
  • Check out the RSA website for free talks and events
  • Sign up to the Bold Tendency mailing list
  • Check out any upcoming events @ Magic Roundabout
  • Know what’s going on in the East: @ East End Citizen
